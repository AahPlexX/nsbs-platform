# Lesson 12: Brand Positioning and Differentiation

## Lesson Overview

This lesson explores the competitive foundations of brand positioning and differentiation, revealing how organizations establish unique market positions that create competitive advantage. The feature-based approach represents the earliest phase of brand positioning thinking. Organizations focus on product attributes and functional benefits to establish market presence. This approach creates visibility that influences initial product consideration while providing baseline understanding of product capabilities.

Consider how the feature-based approach operates across different organizations. In consumer goods, organizations develop brand positioning through product features, quality attributes, and functional benefits to establish market presence. In technology, organizations develop product positioning through technical specifications, performance metrics, and functional capabilities to establish market presence. In services, organizations develop service positioning through service features, quality attributes, and functional benefits to establish market presence. These positioning approaches create visibility that influences initial product consideration while providing baseline understanding.

The feature-based approach has evolved through multiple iterations. Early versions focused on simple product features and functional benefits.
