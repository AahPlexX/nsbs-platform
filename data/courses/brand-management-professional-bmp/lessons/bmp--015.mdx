Cognitive interpretation represents a third form of brand perception. Organizations discover that consumers interpret brand stimuli through existing knowledge structures and mental frameworks, creating meaning-based perception patterns. This cognitive interpretation creates opportunities for brands that align with existing knowledge while challenging brands that conflict with consumer expectations.

Organizations discover that consumers respond positively when brand performance exceeds expectations, creating brand delight and loyalty. This positive violation mechanism creates opportunities for brands that exceed expectations while challenging brands that merely meet expectations.

Negative expectancy violation represents another violation mechanism that influences brand perception. Neutral expectancy violation represents a third violation mechanism that influences brand perception. These expectancy violation mechanisms work together to create comprehensive contrast patterns that influence brand surprise and attention.
